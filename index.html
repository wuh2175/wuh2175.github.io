UC Berkeley · Jan 2026 – Present

Kernel Herding: Implementation & Computational Optimization

Collaboration with a PhD student at UC Berkeley. Implemented the kernel herding algorithm from scratch — a greedy procedure that constructs a deterministic sequence of points whose empirical distribution converges to a target in RKHS. Identified the computational bottleneck in the greedy candidate selection step and proposed a KNN-based approximation to reduce search complexity while preserving sampling quality. Analyzed the approximation-efficiency trade-off empirically.

Reading Group: Advanced Kernel Methods

Systematic study of kernel method theory beyond the PSD assumption, motivated by interest in Prof. Xiaolin Huang's work on indefinite kernels in LS-SVM and random feature approximations. Key question explored: how does the transition from RKHS to Kreĭn space affect optimization guarantees, and what are the implications for algorithms like kernel herding?

Readings

Selected Papers

  • 01

    Indefinite kernels in least squares support vector machines and principal component analysis

    Huang, Maier, Hornegger, Suykens · Appl. Comput. Harmon. Anal. · 2017

    Core paper for my current interest. The positive decomposition K = K₊ − K₋ and Kreĭn space interpretation show that the PSD assumption can be relaxed while preserving computational tractability — raising interesting questions about how this affects algorithms like kernel herding.

  • 02

    Random features for kernel approximation: A survey on algorithms, theory, and beyond

    Liu, Huang, Chen, Suykens · IEEE TPAMI · 2022

    Comprehensive survey connecting random feature methods to kernel approximation. Variance reduction techniques here echo the computational efficiency problems I encountered in my kernel herding work.

  • 03

    Deep kernel learning via random Fourier features

    Xie, Liu, Wang, Huang · arXiv · 2019

    Bridges the deep learning and kernel method worlds — exactly the direction I am most curious about for future research.
